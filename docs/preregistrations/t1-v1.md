## 1. Primary endpoint and primary hypothesis

- **Primary endpoint (T1):** the continuous **property-checklist fraction**
  `score = matched_properties / total_properties ∈ [0, 1]`, computed by the
  frozen deterministic scorer `score_t1_checklist` (R1-3; spec
  `docs/t1-scoring-spec.md`). No LLM judge is used; the scorer is deterministic,
  so two implementers reproduce identical scores.
- **Primary hypothesis:** **H1 on T1 — C4 > C1⁺** ("structure at constant
  information"). This is the central test of the Lumen thesis for the
  understanding task: structured representation helps beyond information
  enrichment.
- **Aggregation:** per-function mean of the score across the two frozen models,
  then paired differences across functions (constitution §protocol; identical to
  the T2 design).
- **Per-model analyses are exploratory only** (no multiplicity budget; reported
  with uncorrected p and labelled exploratory), per constitution v0.3.

---

## 2. Full hypothesis family and multiple-comparison correction

The confirmatory family is the **pre-existing 9-test family** fixed by
Constitution v0.3 and the frozen governance record — **not** a new T1-only
family. T1 supplies three of the nine tests:

| Hypothesis | Comparison | Tasks (family) |
|---|---|---|
| H1 Structure at constant information | C4 > C1⁺ | T1, T2, T3 |
| H2 Total enrichment | C4 > C1 | T1, T2, T3 |
| H3 Information enrichment in text | C1⁺ > C1 | T1, T2, T3 |

- **Family size declared up front: m = 9** (3 hypotheses × 3 tasks).
- **Correction:** Holm–Bonferroni step-down across the family of 9 at α = 0.05,
  exactly as implemented in the frozen `analyze_confirmatory.py`
  (`_holm_bonferroni`, `N_TESTS = 9`).
- **Test:** one-sided Wilcoxon signed-rank, `alternative='greater'`,
  `zero_method='wilcox'` (frozen).
- **Effect size:** rank-biserial correlation `r_rb`, with 95% percentile
  bootstrap CI (seed 20260427, 10 000 resamples; R2-1 amendment, ratified in
  v0.3).
- **Interim-correction caveat (pre-declared):** the family is completed only
  after T3 collection (R5-4). When R5-2 runs the frozen script, the three T3
  cells are still empty and are treated as p = 1.0 (the script's conservative
  default), so the Holm decision for the T1 tests at R5-2 is a valid but
  **provisional** family-wise result. The **final** family-wise correction is
  the one computed once all nine cells are populated (re-run at R5-4). Both the
  provisional (T1+T2 populated) and the raw per-test quantities are reported at
  R5-2; no confirmatory claim flips silently.

## 3. Dataset, ground truth, and power

### 3.1 Dataset

- The **30 retained confirmatory functions** (`dataset_tier = "full"` in
  `data/dataset/functions_manifest.json`). Non-recursive, single-function, as
  enforced by the pipeline exclusion check.
- The 3 held-out pilot anchors (`clamp`, `count_vowels`, `is_sorted`) are
  **excluded** from confirmatory data and never pooled (constitution / paper
  §3.0; R1-5).
- **T1 ground truth authored for R5-1:** one `t1-checklist-v1` artifact per
  confirmatory function under `data/ground_truth/checklists/{func_id}.json`,
  authored from the canonical (correct) source only. Provenance:
  `scripts/author_t1_checklists.py`. The manifest's `tasks.T1.available` is set
  true with `ground_truth_ref` for all 30; `lineage.t1_source_hash` records the
  raw-source hash.

### 3.2 Realised anti-ceiling resolution (R1-3 §4 re-run on the confirmatory set)

The R1-3 anti-ceiling argument is re-run on the **realised** property counts of
the 30 authored checklists (required by `docs/t1-scoring-spec.md` §4):

- Property counts P ∈ {7, 8}; distribution {7: 27, 8: 3}; mean 7.10.
- Minimum per-function attainable levels = min(P) + 1 = **8** (vs the T2
  composite's realised **3** that collapsed effective n).
- Pooled distinct attainable score values across the realised P = **15** in
  [0, 1].
- Self-consistency check: a response echoing all properties scores **1.0** on
  every function; an off-topic response scores **≤ 0.30**. The top of the scale
  is live and no structural ceiling short of 1.0 exists.

This establishes, before collection, that the T1 scorer cannot reproduce the
T2 saturation on this dataset.

### 3.3 Power analysis (Constitution v0.3 decision 5 — required)

Provenance: `scripts/r5_power_analysis.py`. Design: one-sided Wilcoxon
signed-rank on n = 30 paired per-function differences. Method: closed-form
paired z-test n inflated by the Wilcoxon ARE (3/π ≈ 0.955), cross-checked by
4 000-replicate simulation. Effect size = Cohen's `dz` = mean(D)/sd(D) on the
paired differences. Two thresholds: nominal α = 0.05 and the conservative
worst-case Holm step α′ = 0.05/9 = 0.00556.

- **Target power:** 0.80.
- **Minimum n by effect size** (corrected α′ = 0.00556, power 0.80):
  dz = 0.4 → n ≈ 75; dz = 0.5 → n ≈ 48; dz = 0.6 → n ≈ 34; dz = 0.8 → n ≈ 19.
- **At the frozen n = 30:** smallest detectable effect is **dz ≈ 0.63** at the
  corrected threshold (power 0.80) and **dz ≈ 0.47** at nominal α. Simulation:
  power(n=30, dz=0.6) = 0.69 (corrected) / 0.93 (nominal); power(n=30, dz=0.8) =
  0.94 (corrected) / 0.99 (nominal).
- **Reference effect:** the frozen T2/H1 result is r_rb = 0.581 (large),
  implying a probability of superiority ≈ 0.79 and dz ≈ 1.14; n = 30 gives
  power ≈ 1.00 for an effect of that magnitude at either threshold.

**Justification of n = 30 (pre-declared).** n = 30 is the frozen confirmatory
set fixed by governance. It is adequately powered (≥ 0.80) for the
**medium-to-large** paired effects the Lumen thesis predicts for C4 > C1⁺
(dz ≳ 0.63 corrected, ≳ 0.47 nominal), and is powered ≈ 1.0 for an effect the
size of the observed T2/H1 effect. It is **pre-declared as underpowered for
small effects** (dz < 0.5): a medium effect at the corrected threshold would
require n ≈ 48. The dataset is **not** expanded because (a) the 30-function set
is frozen, and (b) the comparison of interest is hypothesised — and, on the
analogous T2 task, observed — to be large. A non-rejection of H1/T1 will
therefore be interpreted in light of this power profile, not as evidence of
absence of a small effect.

  "pilot-v1"`). Wording is identical across conditions; only the representation

## 5. Exclusion rules and pre-declared sensitivity analyses

### 5.1 Exclusion / invalid-response rules (pre-declared)

- An empty/whitespace-only response → `status = invalid_response`,
  `failure_reason.code = empty_response`, and is **not pooled as a 0** (it is
  excluded from the scored set, not counted as an honest zero). This mirrors the
  T3 parse-failure discipline and the L3-05 lesson.
- A `(func, condition, model)` cell with no `status = ok` score is **missing**
  for aggregation; the per-function mean is taken over whichever models are
  present, and complete-pair counts are reported (the frozen analyzer already
  reports `n_complete_pairs` and a completeness table).
- No function is excluded post hoc on the basis of its score.

### 5.2 Sensitivity analyses (pre-declared, run at R5-2)

1. **Missing-not-at-random (MNAR) / invalid-response sensitivity** (R2-2 style):
   report `invalid_response` counts per condition × model; recompute the H1/T1
   test under (a) listwise-complete pairs only and (b) worst-case imputation
   (missing/invalid C4 → 0, missing/invalid C1⁺ → its max) to bound the effect.
   A confirmatory conclusion that flips under worst-case imputation is flagged.
2. **Per-model breakdown** (exploratory): H1/H2/H3 on T1 within each model,
   uncorrected, to expose any model × representation interaction (E5).
3. **Required-only subscore** (exploratory): recompute the fraction over
   `required = true` properties only, to check the result is not driven by
   optional edge-case properties.
